# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

AfroPasso is for Polish dancers, teachers, event organizers, and curious beginners who want to understand African and Afro-diasporic dances with cultural context. The first content focus is the kizomba family: massemba, semba, kizomba, kuduro, tarraxinha, tarraxo, urban kizz, kizomba fusion, and kompa.

Users arrive in an exploratory mood. They want clear differences between styles, a sense of origin, and respectful guidance that connects dance technique with history, music, community, and diaspora.

## Product Purpose

AfroPasso exists to make African dance lineages easier to explore, compare, and respect. It turns research notes into an approachable visual guide with dance cards, search, filters, detail pages, a timeline, a glossary, and explanatory maps.

Success means a visitor quickly understands that these dances are connected to people, places, histories, music, and migration, not just step patterns. The site should feel useful enough for learning and beautiful enough to invite sharing.

## Brand Personality

Warm, rhythmic, and culturally attentive.

The brand voice is human and educational without becoming academic. It should feel like a well-designed cultural guide made by people who love dance, not like a generic database or tourist brochure.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing pages, sterile card grids, heavy nightclub aesthetics, neon cyberpunk, stock-photo exoticism, museum stiffness, and layouts that reduce cultures to decorative patterns. Avoid visual choices that imply all African dances are one interchangeable category.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with people and stories. Technique matters, but the first impression should be connection, movement, and cultural continuity.
2. Explain lineage visually. Use maps, timelines, and relationships to make origins and diaspora legible.
3. Stay warm and readable. The site can be expressive, but learning must remain easy.
4. Respect specificity. Name countries, regions, eras, music influences, controversies, and sources clearly.
5. Build for growth. Kizomba is the first chapter; the structure should support a broader AfroPasso library later.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus states, readable contrast, responsive layouts, and reduced-motion support. Imagery should be respectful, avoid stereotypes, and include useful alt text when used as content.
